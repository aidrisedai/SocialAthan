# Product Requirements Document (PRD)
## Athan + Jama'ah Companion App — v1

**Author:** [Founder]
**Status:** Draft for Design Team
**Last updated:** April 26, 2026
**Target ship date:** ASAP (~3 months from kickoff)

---

## 1. Vision & North Star

Praying in congregation (jama'ah) carries 27 times the reward of praying alone. Yet for many Muslims, the gap between the *intention* to attend the masjid and the *act* of going is wide. Distractions, isolation, and the lack of a connected community can make showing up harder than it should be.

This app helps Muslims close that gap by leveraging brotherhood and sisterhood — the social fabric of the ummah — to gently encourage one another toward consistent attendance at the five daily prayers.

**North Star:** Help Muslims attend the masjid more often by capturing the spiritual and social pull of jama'ah.

**Primary success metric:** Total number of confirmed "going" RSVPs across the user base trends up over time.

**Project ethos:** This product is built as **sadaqah jariyah**. It is free, ad-free, and will never sell user data. Optional subscriptions may be introduced in the future, but v1 is fully free.

---

## 2. Target Users

### 2.1 Primary Persona: The Aspiring Regular
Any Muslim — practicing, returning, or growing — who wants to be present at the masjid for the five daily prayers and would benefit from social encouragement to do so. The app is *not* gated to a particular level of religiosity; it is for anyone with the niyyah (intention) to attend.

### 2.2 Secondary Persona: The Masjid Admin
A representative of a masjid (imam, board member, or designated volunteer) who maintains the masjid's official Adhan and Iqamah times within the app. Multiple admins per masjid are supported.

### 2.3 Tertiary Persona: The Super Admin
Internal team role responsible for backend management — primarily verifying admin claims and updating times for unclaimed masjids when needed.

## 3. Core Principles & Anti-Goals

The design team should treat the following as guardrails. When in doubt, defer to the spirit of these principles.

### 3.1 Principles
- **Ibadah first, engagement second.** This is a tool that serves worship, not the other way around.
- **Asymmetric social visibility.** Positive intentions are celebrated; non-attendance is private. Only "Going" RSVPs are visible to friends. "Maybe" and "Can't make it" responses are never shared with anyone.
- **Trust the user.** The app does not verify attendance. Allah knows the niyyah; the app does not need to police it.
- **Lean v1.** This app does one thing well: prayer times + Adhan + RSVP/social layer. Nothing else.
- **Sadaqah jariyah.** Free forever for users. Never any ads. Never sell data.

### 3.2 Anti-Goals
- ❌ No ads, ever
- ❌ No selling of user data
- ❌ No leaderboards or public rankings (riya concerns)
- ❌ No public shaming or visibility of non-attendance
- ❌ No verification/surveillance of physical attendance
- ❌ No feature creep (no Quran, dhikr, qibla, duas, etc. in v1)

### 3.3 A Note on Streaks
Streaks **are** included in v1 as a positive motivator. The design team should land the tone carefully: streaks should feel like *encouragement to keep going*, not punishment for breaking them. Avoid loss-aversion phrasing ("Don't break your streak!"). Prefer affirmative phrasing ("5 days of Fajr in jama'ah, Alhamdulillah").

## 4. v1 Feature Scope

### 4.1 Prayer Times & Adhan (Utility Layer)

#### Calculation source
- **Default:** Use the Adhan and Iqamah times of the user's **closest** masjid or their **primary** masjid (user's choice in settings).
- **Override:** User may select any standard calculation method (ISNA, MWL, Umm al-Qura, Egyptian, etc.) instead of a masjid's published times.

#### Adhan audio
- Sensible default Adhan (recommend Makkah Adhan).
- User can choose from a small library of reciter options.
- No custom uploads in v1.

#### Notifications (prayer-time related)
- User picks **one or both** for each prayer:
  - Notification at **Adhan time**
  - Notification **10 minutes before Iqamah**
- **Athan audio behavior:** configurable per user/per prayer (full Adhan vs. silent notification).
- **DND override:** Adhan notifications are **time-sensitive / critical alerts** and override Do Not Disturb. Social notifications respect DND.

### 4.2 RSVP / Intention Loop (Heart of the App)

#### The prompt
- User receives a notification **15–30 minutes before Adhan** asking about their intention for the upcoming prayer.

#### Response options
Three states:
- **Going** — visible to friends
- **Maybe** — private
- **Can't make it** — private

#### Behavior
- Responding is **encouraged but not required**. Users can dismiss the prompt without responding.
- After the prayer time passes, the RSVP is automatically marked as **completed** by default. No "did you make it?" follow-up.

### 4.3 Masjid Selection

#### Setup
- During onboarding (and later in settings), users can:
  - Set **one primary masjid**
  - Add **occasional masjids** (e.g., near work, near family, frequent travel destinations)
- The app **auto-suggests** masjids based on the user's current location.

#### Travel behavior
- The app **auto-detects significant location changes** (e.g., user flies to a new city) and re-suggests nearby masjids for ad-hoc selection without disrupting the primary masjid setting.

#### Masjid data source
- **All masjids worldwide** are auto-populated from public data sources (e.g., Google Places).
- Masjid admins can also add a masjid that is missing from the public data.

### 4.4 Community & Social Layer

#### Visibility model (asymmetric, privacy-respecting)
For a given prayer at a given masjid, a user sees:
- **Names and profiles of friends** who have RSVP'd "Going"
- **An aggregate count** of all other users going (e.g., "+18 others going to Maghrib")
- **Nothing** about users who said "Maybe" or "Can't make it" — including friends

#### Community membership
- **Default:** Open. Anyone who selects a masjid is automatically part of that masjid's community for aggregate counts and group features.
- **Optional:** Masjid admins may enable an "approve members" mode for verified-community masjids. (Admin-side flow can be lightweight in v1; designers should specify.)

### 4.5 Friend Connections

#### Discovery & adding
All of the following methods are supported in v1:
- **Contact sync** (find friends already on the app)
- **Username/handle search**
- **QR code** for in-person adds at the masjid (hero interaction — design should make this easy and beautiful)
- **Shareable invite links** (via WhatsApp, SMS, etc.)

### 4.6 Encouragement Mechanics

All of the following are supported and granularly controllable by the user:
- **Pre-set duas / reactions** — quick taps like "May Allah accept," "See you there," "Barak Allahu feek"
- **Direct messages** between connected friends
- **Group chat per masjid** — community feed for users attending a specific prayer
- **Nudge button** — sends a gentle "hoping to see you" notification to a friend

#### Smart proactive notifications
The app surfaces social proof notifications proactively, but **scaled to the user's recent engagement**:
- **Active users:** May receive notifications like "3 of your friends are going to Isha at Masjid Al-Noor"
- **Less active / dormant users:** Receive fewer, gentler nudges to avoid overwhelm on return
- All proactive notifications are configurable and opt-in.

### 4.7 Streaks
- Personal, private streak tracking for consistent attendance (e.g., 5 days of Fajr in jama'ah).
- No public visibility, no leaderboards.
- Positive framing only.

### 4.8 Jummah Handling
- Jummah **replaces Dhuhr** automatically on Fridays.
- Single Jummah time per masjid in v1. (Multi-service masjids — i.e., masjids with two or three Jummah congregations — are deferred to v2.)

### 4.9 Notification Controls
Granular settings:
- Master on/off
- Per category (Adhan, Iqamah reminder, friend RSVPs, encouragements, nudges, streak reminders)
- Per prayer (e.g., Fajr only, no Dhuhr)

## 5. Onboarding Flow

### 5.1 Goal
Get the user from app launch to a working Adhan + RSVP experience in **under 60 seconds**, with no forced social setup.

### 5.2 Steps
1. **Open the app** → brief value-prop intro screen
2. **Grant location permission**
3. **See nearby masjids** (auto-suggested from location)
4. **Select primary masjid** (one tap)
5. **Default name assigned** (user can edit, but not required)
6. **Allow notifications** prompt
7. **Enter the app** — fully functional Adhan + prayer time experience

### 5.3 Deferred (post-onboarding)
- Profile completion (photo, real name, bio)
- Adding occasional masjids
- Friend invites
- Notification fine-tuning

### 5.4 Empty State (No Friends Yet)
- Show **aggregate masjid counts** immediately so the app feels alive (e.g., "23 going to Maghrib at Masjid Al-Noor")
- Surface **invite CTAs** to share to existing groups (WhatsApp, contacts, SMS)
- Encourage **QR code add** at the masjid

## 6. Masjid Admin Experience (v1)

### 6.1 Capabilities
Masjid admins can do **only** the following in v1:
- Set and update **Adhan times** for each of the five prayers (and Jummah)
- Set and update **Iqamah times** for each of the five prayers (and Jummah)

That's it. No announcements, no chat moderation, no analytics, no member approval (except the optional toggle from §4.4), no push notifications to congregation.

### 6.2 Claiming a Masjid
- Any user can initiate a claim for a masjid.
- **Multiple admins per masjid** are allowed.
- Verification process is required (specifics TBD — recommended: email match to masjid's official domain, or a manual review by super admin).

### 6.3 Unclaimed Masjids
- Display **calculated prayer times** (no custom Iqamah).
- Show a **"Claim this masjid"** CTA visible to users.
- **Super admin** can update times manually for unclaimed masjids when needed.

## 7. User Stories (Selected, Non-Exhaustive)

- *As a Muslim*, I want to be reminded of prayer 15-30 minutes before Adhan so I can plan to make it to the masjid in time.
- *As a Muslim*, I want to see which of my friends are going to the next prayer at my masjid so I'm motivated to join them.
- *As a Muslim*, I want to RSVP "Maybe" privately without anyone seeing, so I'm not pressured into commitments I'm unsure about.
- *As a Muslim*, I want to send a quick "May Allah accept" to a friend who RSVP'd, so I can encourage them without typing.
- *As a traveler*, I want the app to detect I'm in a new city and suggest nearby masjids automatically.
- *As a new user*, I want to start using the app for prayer times in under a minute, without setting up a profile or finding friends first.
- *As a masjid admin*, I want to update our Iqamah times in seconds when our schedule changes seasonally.
- *As a user adding a friend at the masjid*, I want to scan their QR code in person rather than fumbling with usernames.

## 8. Edge Cases & Special Behaviors

| Scenario | v1 Behavior |
|---|---|
| User flies to a new city | Auto-detect, re-suggest nearby masjids |
| Friday | Jummah replaces Dhuhr automatically |
| User RSVP's "Going" but prayer time passes | Mark as completed by default |
| User declines location permission | Allow manual masjid selection by search |
| User has no friends on the app | Show aggregate counts + invite CTAs |
| Masjid has no admin | Show calculated times + "Claim this masjid" CTA |
| User wants Adhan silent during work hours | Configurable per prayer |
| Adhan during Do Not Disturb | Override DND (time-sensitive) |
| Social notification during DND | Respect DND |

## 9. Out of Scope for v1 (Explicitly Deferred)

The following are **intentionally not in v1**. The design team should not build for them, but should design in a way that doesn't preclude them.

- ❌ Qibla direction
- ❌ Quran reader, dhikr counter, duas library, Hijri calendar
- ❌ Ramadan-specific features (Taraweeh, Suhoor/Iftar timing)
- ❌ Goal-setting (e.g., "I want to attend Fajr 3x/week"). Streaks serve as implicit goals in v1.
- ❌ Attendance verification (GPS check-in, QR scan at masjid, post-prayer "did you make it?")
- ❌ Multi-service Jummah handling (multiple congregations at one masjid)
- ❌ Multiple Iqamahs per prayer per masjid
- ❌ Women's facilities indicators / gendered RSVPs
- ❌ Apple Watch / Wear OS apps
- ❌ Tablet / iPad optimization
- ❌ Languages beyond English
- ❌ Geographic markets beyond US
- ❌ Specialized accessibility features beyond OS standards
- ❌ Masjid admin features beyond Adhan/Iqamah times (announcements, analytics, chat moderation, push to congregation)
- ❌ Subscriptions / paid features

## 11. Visual & Brand Direction

- **Aesthetic:** **Modern minimalist.** Clean typography, generous whitespace, thoughtful use of color. Closer to Calm or a great calendar app than to traditional Islamic-app aesthetics.
- **Avoid:** Heavy ornate calligraphy, kitschy mosque illustrations, the green-and-gold cliché.
- **Tone:** Warm, sincere, respectful. The product should feel like a supportive friend, not a productivity tool and not a religious authority.

## 12. Success Metrics

### 12.1 North Star
- **Total RSVPs marked "Going" per week** (trending up = product is working)

### 12.2 Supporting Metrics
- RSVP response rate (% of prompts that get a response)
- Average streak length per active user
- Friend connections per active user
- Masjids claimed by admins (% of masjids in markets served)
- Notification opt-in rate (especially Adhan and pre-Iqamah)
- Day 7 / Day 30 retention
- Invites sent and accepted

### 12.3 Anti-Metrics (we are NOT optimizing for these)
- Time spent in app
- Daily session count
- Engagement that doesn't lead to attendance

## 13. Open Questions for Design Team

These are unresolved items the design team should address during the design phase:

1. **Default name format:** What format should the auto-assigned default name take? (e.g., "Brother 1234," "Guest A47K," something else?)
2. **Admin verification:** What is the lightest-touch verification flow that prevents abuse but doesn't block legitimate masjid admins?
3. **Aggregate count threshold:** At what number does "X going" become motivating vs. discouraging? Should there be a minimum threshold before showing a count?
4. **Group chat moderation:** With no admin moderation in v1, how do we handle abuse in masjid group chats? (Recommended: simple report/block for v1, defer richer tools.)
5. **Notification timing personalization:** Should the 15-30 minute Adhan window be user-configurable, or fixed per prayer based on commute distance to primary masjid?
6. **QR code design:** Static or rotating? Privacy considerations of in-person adds.
7. **Onboarding without notification permission:** What is the experience if a user denies notifications? The app's core loop depends on them.
8. **Gentle re-engagement copy:** What does the "depending on last app use" smart notification logic actually say? Sample copy needed.

## 15. Appendix: Glossary

- **Adhan / Athan:** The Islamic call to prayer
- **Iqamah:** The second call indicating prayer is about to begin in congregation
- **Jama'ah:** Congregational prayer
- **Jummah:** Friday congregational prayer (replaces Dhuhr)
- **Masjid:** Mosque
- **Niyyah:** Intention
- **Sadaqah jariyah:** Ongoing charity that continues to benefit others over time
- **Riya:** Performing acts of worship for the sake of being seen by others (to be avoided)
